# Automation Menu

A starting list of things your ShadowDesk OS can do for you, grouped by where they live in your business. Read this with Claude when you don't know what to automate next. Pick one. Claude (via `/skill-builder`) will build it.

These are starting points, not a checklist. Every example below is a real, finishable skill — not a category, not a feature. If your version of the work is a little different, that's fine — Claude shapes the skill around how YOU actually do it.

---

## Revenue

*How money comes in — billing, invoicing, getting paid.*

- **Follow up on unpaid invoices.** Pull every open invoice over a number of days you pick, draft a polite reminder in your voice, and queue them as drafts for you to send.
- **Recap last month's revenue.** Add up what came in, compare to the month before, flag anything weird (a missed renewal, a one-time spike), and write a 5-line summary you can paste into your weekly journal.
- **Nudge an overdue payment.** When a customer is X days late, draft the second-touch message that's firmer than the first but still warm — never the same line twice.
- **Send a proposal.** Take your standard proposal, fill in the customer's name, scope, and price, and queue it as a draft.

---

## Customers

*Finding, talking to, onboarding, and keeping the people who pay you.*

- **Sweep customers who've gone quiet.** Surface anyone you haven't heard from in 30/60/90 days, with a one-line "here's where you left off" so you can pick the right one to restart.
- **Restart a stalled conversation.** Take a prospect who ghosted and draft a fresh, no-guilt opener that references the last real thing you talked about.
- **Run the first week of onboarding.** Schedule the kickoff, send the welcome email, drop a folder into `clients/`, and queue the first check-in for day 5.
- **Recap your relationship with one customer.** Pull every call, email, and note you have about one person and produce a one-page "where we are" briefing before your next conversation with them.
- **Capture a new customer.** When a deal closes, create the customer folder, log the deal details, and write the first entry of their history.

---

## Calendar

*Where your time goes — recurring meetings, prep, blocks.*

- **Draft your morning briefing.** A 60-second readout of today's calendar, what each meeting is for, who's coming, and the one thing to remember for each.
- **Prep for your next meeting.** Pull the customer's history, recent emails, your last commitments to them, and a 3-bullet "what good looks like in this meeting."
- **Review the week behind you.** Sunday or Friday, look back at what actually happened on the calendar versus what you said you'd do, and ask one question that surfaces what to fix.
- **Audit where your time went.** Add up meeting hours by category (sales, delivery, internal, personal) and show the number against last week so you can see drift.

---

## Communication

*Email, texts, DMs — what eats your time.*

- **Triage the inbox.** Read everything that came in overnight, sort by who needs you today vs. this week vs. never, and draft 2-line responses for the easy ones.
- **Draft this reply.** Paste an email you've been avoiding; get back a draft in your voice that handles it directly without sounding rushed.
- **Write the weekly recap.** Once a week, produce the "here's what happened, here's what's next" note you'd send to a partner, an investor, your team, or yourself.
- **Batch through a pile.** When 15 messages have piled up, work them one by one with you — Claude opens each, drafts the response, you approve or edit, next.

---

## Operations

*The repeating back-office admin that keeps the business running.*

- **Close out the month.** Reconcile what came in, what went out, what's outstanding, and produce a one-page month-end snapshot.
- **Run your recurring admin.** The renewals, the subscriptions to check, the reports to pull — whatever you do on the 1st and the 15th, handed off to a skill that runs the list.
- **Categorize expenses.** Take this month's card statement, sort every line into the right business category, flag the weird ones for you to look at.
- **Write a status update.** When a partner, investor, or team member asks "how's it going," produce the honest 5-line answer with the right level of detail for who's asking.

---

## Meetings

*Calls, prep, recaps, where notes and recordings end up.*

- **Write up the meeting that just ended.** The canonical first skill. Pull the transcript from your recorder, produce a summary, list the action items, draft the follow-up email, file everything in the right customer folder.
- **Brief you before the meeting.** 30 minutes before a call, hand you who they are, what you talked about last time, what you said you'd do, and the one question that opens the conversation.
- **Pull out the action items.** Read a transcript or set of notes and produce just the list of "things someone said they'd do" — with who, when, and what.
- **Follow up with everyone who was there.** After a multi-person meeting, draft a different follow-up to each attendee based on what THEY specifically committed to.

---

## Knowledge

*Where your business know-how lives — processes, templates, materials.*

- **Capture a process.** Walk Claude through how you do something once; get back a written playbook you (or a future hire) can follow.
- **Update a playbook.** When the way you do something changes, find the existing playbook, edit the right section, and date-stamp the change.
- **Pull a template out of an example.** Paste three of the best emails (or proposals, or pitches) you've ever written; get back a reusable template that captures the pattern.
- **Log a decision.** When you make a non-obvious call ("I'm raising prices to X" / "I'm dropping this product"), record what you decided, why, and what you'd watch for to know if it was wrong.

---

## How to use this menu

When you don't know what to build next, open this file and read it with Claude. Pick one example that would actually save you time this week — not the most exciting one, the most repetitive one. Then say: "Let's build this." Claude will route you into `/skill-builder` and you'll have it working in one session.

The menu grows over time. As you build new skills, your ShadowDesk OS gets better at suggesting the next one. If something belongs here that isn't, tell Claude — it can add the row.
