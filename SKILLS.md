# SKILLS

The skill catalog for this ShadowDesk OS. One entry per skill: what it does, when to invoke it, what to listen for in my messages.

> Auto-regenerated by `/shadowdesk:end-session`. Read this before responding to my first message in any session.

Status legend: `[live]` = built and ready. `[not yet built]` = placeholder; the skill is on the roadmap but not on disk yet.

---

## The ShadowDesk plugin

The full skill library came bundled with your ShadowDesk OS and switched on during Day One. It lives under `/shadowdesk:` commands. Type `/shadowdesk:` in chat to see them all. Two to know first: `/shadowdesk:adapt <skill>` wires a skill to my tools, and `/shadowdesk:update` pulls Nick's latest improvements (that one needs your personal key; until you have it, you're on the free starter set).

Three skills that used to live in this folder now live there: `/shadowdesk:brainstorming`, `/shadowdesk:grill-me`, and `/shadowdesk:end-session`.

## `/skill-builder` — build a new skill for a recurring task

**Status:** `[live]`

**Purpose.** The compositional engine that grows new skills. Routes to `/shadowdesk:grill-me` when I have a task in mind, or `/shadowdesk:brainstorming` when I don't. Captures how long the task takes me by hand, blocks on missing connections or missing voice profile, generates the skill, tests it once with a real record, and updates this catalog.

**Invoke when I say:**
- "build me a skill"
- "automate this"
- "I keep doing X by hand"
- "I want a skill for Y"
- "what should I automate next"
- or paste a recurring task with any "turn this into a skill" framing

## `/begin-session` — load a topic and brief me

**Status:** `[live]`

**Purpose.** Topic-loader. Resolves a named client / project / initiative to its folder under `shadowdesk/clients/` or `shadowdesk/projects/`, fans out in parallel across the folder + auto-memory + wired connectors (Gmail, Calendar, HubSpot, Otter, Notion, Drive), and hands back a tight chat-only briefing. Empty sections get omitted entirely (no filler). Bare invocation surfaces a recent-folders menu.

**Invoke when I say:**
- "load up Atlas Brewery for me"
- "get me up to speed on Q4 Pricing"
- "where are we with <client>"
- "/begin-session" (with or without a topic)
- or I open the session by deeply engaging a specific named topic

**Do NOT invoke** for quick tasks or general questions — those don't need background context.
