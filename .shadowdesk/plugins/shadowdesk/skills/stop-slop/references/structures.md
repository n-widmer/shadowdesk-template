# Structures to avoid

From [hardikpandya/stop-slop](https://github.com/hardikpandya/stop-slop) (MIT).

## Binary contrasts
False drama through negation-based reversals. State your point directly.
Examples: "Not because X. Because Y." / "[X] isn't the problem. [Y] is." / "The answer isn't X. It's Y." / "It feels like X. It's actually Y." / "The question isn't X. It's Y." / "Not X. But Y." / "stops being X and starts being Y" / "doesn't mean X, but actually Y" / "not just X but also Y"
**Fix:** State Y directly. Drop the negation entirely.

## Negative listing
Don't list what something isn't before revealing what it is.
Examples: "Not a X... Not a Y... A Z." / "It wasn't X. It wasn't Y. It was Z."
**Fix:** State the thing directly without the runway.

## Dramatic fragmentation
Sentence fragments for emphasis read as manufactured profundity.
Examples: "[Noun]. That's it." / "X. And Y. And Z." / "This unlocks something. [Word]."
**Fix:** Complete sentences; trust content over presentation.

## Rhetorical setups
Announce insight rather than deliver it: "What if [reframe]?" / "Here's what I mean:" / "Think about it:" / "And that's okay."
**Fix:** Make the point directly.

## Formulaic constructions
"By the time X, I was Y." / "X that isn't Y"

## False agency
Giving inanimate things human verbs removes human actors from responsibility.
Examples: "a complaint becomes a fix" / "a bet lives or dies" / "the decision emerges" / "the culture shifts" / "the conversation moves toward" / "the data tells us" / "the market rewards"
**Fix:** Name the human actor doing the action.

## Narrator-from-a-distance
Floating above scenes instead of positioning readers inside them.
Examples: "Nobody designed this." / "This happens because..." / "People tend to..."
**Fix:** Direct address: "You don't sit down one day and decide to..."

## Passive voice
Hides the actor and drains energy: "X was created" / "It is believed that" / "Mistakes were made."
**Fix:** Name who performed the action; lead with the actor.

## Sentence starters to avoid
Wh- words, "So," "Look," questions with immediate answers.
**Fix:** Lead with subject or verb; restructure for directness.

## Rhythm patterns
Avoid three-item lists, every paragraph ending punchily, em dashes, staccato fragmentation, "Not always. Not perfectly."
**Fix:** Vary rhythms; two items instead of three; commas or periods instead of em dashes.

## Word patterns
Avoid lazy extremes (every, always, never, everyone, nobody) and "-ly" crutch adverbs.
**Fix:** Use specifics; remove empty emphasis words.
