# Ask — monetize without killing the goose (optional)

Source: *$100M Leads*, Ch. 7 ("Post Free Content Part II"). **Default to no ask.** This reference only matters when the user explicitly wants a CTA on a given run. Read it then.

---

## The give : ask ratio

Gary Vaynerchuk's "jab, jab, jab, right hook" — give many times before you ask. "You deposit goodwill with rewarding content, then withdraw from it by making offers."

Studied ratios: TV runs ~3.5:1 give-to-ask, Facebook ~4 posts per ad. But those are mature platforms that "care less about growing their audiences and care more about making money from them." Growing platforms "dramatically over give and under ask."

> "If you want to grow an audience, give far far more than you ask."

**Hormozi's preferred strategy — give until they ask:** "People are always waiting for you to ask for money. And when you don't, they trust you more. They share your stuff more. You grow faster... If you give enough, people start asking you... give in public, ask in private. You let the audience self-select when they're ready to give you money."

> Bottom line: "The moment you start asking for money is the moment you decide to slow down your growth. So the more patient you are, the more you will get when you finally make your ask."

This is why the skill defaults to pure give. Only append a CTA when the user asks for one.

---

## If you do ask: two ways to weave it in

"Think of 'asks' as commercials. You interrupt this program with a very important message."

- **Integrated** — an ad inside the content, "so long as you keep your give : ask ratio high." (An hour podcast with 3×30-second ads is still 58.5 min give to 1.5 min ask, well above 3:1.) Warning: a friend's podcast that asked too frequently *in* the content stopped growing and shrank. "Don't kill your golden goose." Place the CTA **"after a valuable moment or the end of the content piece."**
- **Intermittent** — pure-give posts, then occasionally a dedicated "ask" post (e.g. 10 give posts, the 11th promotes). "On short platforms, the intermittent way will dominate. On long-form platforms, integrations are often your best bet."

For a single content unit, the default when an ask is wanted is **one integrated CTA at the end.**

---

## What to advertise — pick ONE

"When you make your ask, you either advertise your core offer, or you advertise your lead magnet. That's it. Don't overcomplicate this." If unsure, **do the lead magnet — it's lower risk.**

**Lead magnet (lower risk):** offer more of the same value in exchange for an opt-in.
> "I have 11 more tips that have helped me do this. Go to my site to grab a pretty visual of them." (Then the thank-you page after opt-in shows the paid offer. "Bonus points if your lead magnet is relevant to your content advertising it.")

**Core offer (go for the jugular):** straight to the sale, modeled on the Grand Slam Offer shape:
> "I'm looking for 5 (specific avatar) to help achieve (dream outcome) in (time delay). The best part is, you don't have to (effort and sacrifice). And if you don't get (dream outcome), I will do two things: 1) I will hand you your money back 2) I will work with you until you do. I do this because I want everyone to have an amazing experience with us and because I'm confident I can deliver on my promise. If that sounds fair, DM me / book a call / comment below / reply to this email / etc."

After the ask: "get back to providing value."

When you build a CTA for the user, fill the avatar / dream outcome / time delay / effort from the client's real offer — never fabricate an offer or a guarantee. If the offer details aren't known, ask before writing the ask.
