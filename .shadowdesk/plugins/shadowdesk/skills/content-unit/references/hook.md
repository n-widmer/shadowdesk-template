# Hook — get them to start (Topics + Headlines + Format)

Source: *$100M Leads*, Ch. 6 ("Post Free Content Part I"). The hook's job: "give them a reason to redirect their attention from whatever they are doing towards us." Its effectiveness is "measured by the percentage of people who start consuming your content." You increase that percentage three ways: **picking topics they find interesting, headlines that give them a reason, and matching the format of other stuff they like.**

---

## Topics — the five categories

Hormozi prefers personal experience: "there's only one of you. The easiest way to differentiate is to say something no one else can say. And no one else has lived your life but you." Place the input in one of these:

- **Far Past** — "The important past lessons in your life. Connect that wisdom to your product or service to provide huge value to your audience. Give them the story without the scar." (His phone-usage story is the model: complaint → friend pulls up 3 hrs/day of social → "Hey, I found you some time." A simple, relatable story that ties his work, growing businesses, to a struggle many people have, not enough time.)
- **Recent Past** — "Do stuff, then talk about what you did (or what happened)." Mine the last week of meetings, conversations, and warm reach-outs. "There's gold in these conversations." Testimonials and case studies live here: "If you can tell a cool client story in a way that provides value to your audience, you'll both promote your services and provide value. Win-win."
- **Present** — "Write down ideas at the exact time they come to you." He notes ideas publicly as they happen; if a post does better than normal, that's a signal to make more on that topic.
- **Trending** — "Go where the attention is. Look at what's trending right now and make stuff about it. Apply your own experiences to it." Effective for reaching a broader audience.
- **Manufactured** — "Turn your ideas into reality. Pick a topic people find interesting. Then, learn about it, make it, or do it. Then, show it to the world." Costs the most (you create the experience) but "can have the biggest payouts." (e.g. "I lived on $100 for a month. Here's how.")

**Action step:** "Life happens — profit by sharing yours."

## Headlines — use AT LEAST TWO of these seven

"A headline is a short phrase or sentence used to grab the audience's attention. It communicates the reason they should consume the content." Hormozi gives the timeless principles over templates, from a meta-analysis of news. **"Try and include at least two in your headline."**

- **Recency** — "As recent as possible, quite literally the 'new's." (Something an hour ago beats a year ago.)
- **Relevancy** — "Personally meaningful." (Nurses care about nurse stuff, not accountant stuff.)
- **Celebrity** — "Including prominent people (celebrities, authorities, etc.)." (Nobody cares what a stranger eats for breakfast; they care what Jeff Bezos eats.)
- **Proximity** — "Close to home — geographically." (A house fire across the country gets nothing; your neighbor's gets your full attention.)
- **Conflict** — "of opposing ideas, opposing people, nature, etc." (Pineapple vs. no pineapple. Good vs. Evil. Freedom vs. Security.)
- **Unusual** — "odd, unique, rare, bizarre." (The six-fingered man at the circus.)
- **Ongoing** — "Stories still in progress are dynamic, evolving, and have plot twists." (Someone goes into labor and people want an update every ten minutes.)

**Action step:** "Include one or more of these components to give yourself meatier, attention-grabbing headlines." (Aim for two-plus.)

---

## Format — look like what already won on the platform

"We need to match our format to the best content on the platform. People consume content because it's similar to stuff they've liked in the past. And matching the popular format of the platform gets the most people to interact with it."

> "You've gotta make your content look like what they expect will reward them. Otherwise, no matter how good it is, better-looking content will hook them before yours even has a chance."

**Action step:** "Format your content for the platform first. Then, tweak it so it hooks your ideal audience. Use the best content on the platform that targets your market as your guide."

So: LinkedIn reads differently than X reads differently than a caption. Shape the hook to the platform named in the run before tuning it to the audience.
